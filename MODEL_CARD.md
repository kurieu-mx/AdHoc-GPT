# Model Card — AdHoc-GPT

AdHoc-GPT is a family of small decoder-only language models built from scratch in
PyTorch for this repository. Three checkpoints are produced by the pipeline:

| checkpoint | phase | init | training data | purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `runs/mini-adhoc-lm` | 1 | random | tiny-Shakespeare, character-level | prove the architecture and training loop |
| `runs/mini-tinystories` | 2 | random | TinyStories slice, byte-level BPE | scale to a real corpus and a subword vocabulary |
| `runs/adhoc-lm-domain` | 3 | `mini-tinystories` | synthetic diplomacy corpus, same BPE vocab | domain specialization: resolutions, debate, position papers |

## Results

Single RTX 4070 Laptop (8 GB), bf16 mixed precision.

| run | params | vocab | context | tokens seen | best val loss | wall clock |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `mini-adhoc-lm` | 7.48M | 65 (char) | 256 | 82M | 1.4528 | 28.9 min |
| `mini-tinystories` | 8.11M | 2048 (BPE) | 256 | 74M | 1.5990 | 23.9 min |
| `adhoc-lm-domain` | 8.11M | 2048 (BPE) | 256 | — | *(training)* | — |

Losses are not comparable across rows: they are cross-entropy per *token*, and the
tokenizers differ (a character and a BPE token carry different amounts of information).

## Architecture

Decoder-only transformer, pre-norm residual blocks, learned positional
embeddings, weight-tied output head. `mini` preset: 6 layers, 8 heads, 320
embedding dimensions, 256-token context, ~7.5M parameters (character vocab).
Attention, LayerNorm, GELU and the BPE tokenizer are all implemented from first
principles in this repo; see `README.md` for the component map.

## Intended use

Educational and portfolio use: understanding transformer internals, tokenizer
construction, training dynamics, retrieval-augmented generation, and the shape
of a pretrain → fine-tune → application pipeline.

## Out of scope

- **Not a source of factual information.** At this scale the models produce
  fluent-looking text with no reliable factual grounding.
- **Not authentic multilateral practice.** The domain model is trained on a
  *synthetic, templated* corpus generated by `adhoc_gpt/domain/corpus.py`. Its
  output imitates the register of UN drafting; it does not reproduce real
  resolutions, real negotiating positions, or the practice of any State or
  organ. Drafts carry a disclaimer in both the CLI and the web UI, and that
  disclaimer should travel with any output that leaves this repo.
- **Not for deployment in any decision-making process**, diplomatic or otherwise.

## Training data

- **tiny-Shakespeare** (1.1M characters) — public domain, downloaded at prep time.
- **TinyStories** (`roneneldan/TinyStories`, a capped slice) — synthetic
  children's stories, used as general-English pretraining.
- **Synthetic diplomacy corpus** — generated in-repo from clause grammars over
  eight topics (climate resilience, nuclear disarmament, refugee protection,
  cybersecurity, AI governance, food security, maritime security, global
  health). Deterministic given a seed; no real documents are included.

## Limitations and biases

The domain corpus is a *template*, so the model learns the surface form of
multilateral drafting — participial preambular clauses, numbered operative
paragraphs — rather than the substance of negotiation. Topic coverage, the list
of delegations, and the framing of each issue are all authored choices in
`corpus.py`; a model trained on it inherits those choices. Country names appear
in the corpus purely as drafting scaffolding, and generated text should never be
read as a position attributable to any State. Retrieval surfaces clauses from
that same synthetic library, so it improves format fidelity, not factuality.

## Evaluation

Validation cross-entropy on a held-out 10% split of each corpus, reported in
each run's `metrics.csv` and `summary.json` and plotted in `training_curves.png`.
No downstream task benchmark: at this scale the loss curve and qualitative
samples are the honest evaluation.
